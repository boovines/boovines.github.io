About Me

I'm a Computer Science major at Columbia University (Class of 2027), where I'm an I.I. Rabi Scholar with a 4.0 major GPA. On campus, I lead the Almaworks Accelerator and serve on the executive board of CORE, organizing Columbia's largest startup demo day and mentoring early-stage founders. I'm passionate about creating real impact through entrepreneurship and tech.

I've worked on machine learning models for tissue segmentation and solar activity prediction, interning at Lockheed Martin and Columbia's Morphogenomics Lab. My projects involve transformers, GCNs, and RNNs, and I build in Python, PyTorch, TensorFlow, Java, and C. I've also scraped founder data with Crunchbase APIs and built full-stack apps using TypeScript and Node.js. I'm deeply interested in the intersection of technology and finance, and I'll be joining UBS Investment Banking next summer.

In my free time, you'll find me street-busking on guitar, working through chess puzzles, or playing water polo and golf. I'm always excited by new ideas, creative people, and the chance to build something that lasts.
